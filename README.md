## 📂 Importing your documents

The software keeps your files organized. Create a folder on your computer where you keep your study notes or project work. Open the Langent application. Drag your folder into the interface. The tool reads the files and indexes the content. This happens locally on your computer. Your data stays private.

## 🌌 Using the 3D visualizer

The app generates a visual structure of your files. You see nodes connected by lines. Each node represents a piece of information. Close nodes indicate related topics. You click on a node to read the content. Use your mouse to rotate the structure and zoom in for details. This helps you spot links between documents that you might overlook.

## 🔗 Connecting to AI tools

The Model Context Protocol connects your local index to AI platforms. 

1. Launch your AI application like Cursor or an MCP-supported chat client.
2. Open the settings menu in the AI app.
3. Look for the connections or integrations tab.
4. Add a new server.
5. Select the path where you installed the Langent kit.
6. The AI now sees your document index when you ask questions.

## 🔍 Refining your search

The system updates as you change your documents. If you add a new PDF, the software detects the file. Use the search bar at the top of the interface. Type a keyword. The application highlights the relevant nodes in the 3D cloud. This method makes finding exact quotes or facts fast.

## 🛠 Troubleshooting common issues

**The app does not open**
Restart your computer. Ensure your antivirus software does not block the application. You might need to grant permission in the Windows security settings.

**The 3D view appears empty**
Check that your folder contains readable file types like PDF, TXT, or MD. Refresh the library from the settings menu.

**AI tools do not see my files**
Check the connection path in your AI software. Ensure the Langent application runs in the background. Close and restart your AI client to refresh settings.

## 📜 How logic works

The software uses advanced indexing methods. It turns text into vectors. These are numerical values that represent the meaning of your words. When you search, the software compares your query to these numbers. This provides results based on context rather than simple word matching. The 3D cloud maps these connections to show the breadth of your knowledge base.

## 🏢 Privacy and data safety

Your data stays on your machine. The software processes files locally. It does not upload your private documents to external servers. Only the metadata necessary for the AI tool to perform retrieval moves through the connection protocol. You keep full control over which folders the software scans. Remove a folder from the settings to delete the index records for those files.

## 💡 Workflow tips

Sort your documents by project. Use clear folders to help the software group information. Keep notes in Markdown files for the best results. Update your index regularly as your document collection grows. The system scales to support thousands of files without losing speed.
